Lasagna recipe


This is a Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made of layers of pasta sheets, rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese, all baked together to perfection. Each layer soaks up the flavors of the sauce, creating a comforting, hearty meal that's both flavorful and satisfying.

History of lasagna

Originally from the region of Emilia-Romagna in Italy, lasagna has become a beloved dish around the world, with countless variations — from traditional beef ragu to spinach and ricotta or even vegan versions. Its golden, cheesy top and soft, flavorful layers underneath make it a timeless favorite for family dinners and celebrations alike.


Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles (pasta sheets) - form the layers of the dish.
  • Meat or tomato sauce - adds flavor and richness.
  • Cheese (usually mozzarella or ricotta) - gives it that creamy, melted texture.

Steps for preparation

  1. Cook the ingredients – Boil the lasagna noodles and prepare the meat or tomato sauce.
  2. Layer the lasagna – In a baking dish, alternate layers of sauce, noodles, and cheese.
  3. Bake – Cook in the oven until the top is golden and bubbly.
