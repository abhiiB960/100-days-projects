Persimmon bread

Persimmon bread is a moist, sweet loaf made from ripe persimmons that give it a rich flavor and vibrant color. It’s warmly spiced, soft in texture, and perfect for cozy breakfasts or afternoon snacks.

History

Persimmon bread has its roots in American Southern and Asian traditions, where ripe persimmons were often used in seasonal baking. Over time, it became a cherished autumn treat, celebrated for turning simple, homegrown fruit into comforting, fragrant loaves.

Ingredients

  • Ripe persimmons – provide natural sweetness and flavor.
  • Flour – forms the base of the bread.
  • Sugar – adds sweetness and enhances the fruit’s taste.

Steps for preparation

  1. Prepare the persimmons – Scoop out the pulp from ripe persimmons and mash it smoothly.
  2. Mix the batter – Combine the persimmon pulp with flour, sugar, and other ingredients like eggs and spices.
  3. Bake – Pour the batter into a loaf pan and bake until golden brown and cooked through.
